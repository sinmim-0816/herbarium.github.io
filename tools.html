Essential Herbarium Tools

Perfect every specimen with the right tools



Collecting Essentials

Tools to Gather Specimens in the Field

Scissor

Used to carefully cut plant specimens without damaging important parts like leaves or flowers.

Sealable Plastic Bags

Keeps freshly collected plant specimens safe and moist during transportation, preventing them from drying out too quickly.

Field Notebook

Essential for recording important details about each plant specimen, including its location, habitat, and collection date.

GPS Device or Smartphone

Helps to accurately track the location where each specimen is collected for future reference and research.

Sturdy Bag

Used to carry all the collected specimens and tools securely while in the field.

Preparation Basics

Getting Specimens Ready for Preservation

Thin Plywood or Plant Press

Used to flatten and dry plant specimens without losing their shape. The plywood helps in creating pressure while the plants are arranged between layers of absorbent material.

Parchment Paper

Acts as a protective layer between plant specimens, helping them dry evenly without sticking to other materials.

Cooler or Refrigerator (for temporary storage)

Keeps freshly collected plant specimens cool and moist, slowing down the degradation process before they can be pressed and preserved.

Pressing Techniques

Drying and Preserving Plant Structures

Parchment Paper

Acts as a protective layer between plant specimens, helping them dry evenly without sticking to other materials.

Plant Press or Heavy Books

Mounting Mastery

Displaying and Protecting Your Specimens

Acid-Free Paper

Used as a base for mounting plant specimens. This special paper ensures long-term preservation by preventing chemical reactions that could deteriorate the specimen.

Acrylic Adhesive

A safe, clear glue used to affix plant specimens to the mounting paper. It holds specimens securely without damaging them over time.

Tweezers

Precision tools for handling and positioning delicate plant parts during the mounting process, ensuring accuracy without causing damage.

Freezing Protection

Pest Prevention for Long-Term Storage

Plastic Wrap

Wraps specimens securely for freezing, preventing moisture loss and ensuring the plants maintain their original form during the freezing process.

Identification Tools

Accurate Classification Methods

Hand Lens

A magnifying glass used to closely examine small details of plants, such as leaf veins, hairs, or flower parts, that are critical for accurate identification.

Dichotomous Key

A tool that helps identify plant species by guiding users through a series of choices based on physical characteristics of the specimen.

Cataloging System

Organizing and Preserving Your Collection

Acid-Free Sleeves

Protective covers that store mounted specimens, preventing them from SSexposure to light, dust, or chemicals that could damage the plant material over time.
